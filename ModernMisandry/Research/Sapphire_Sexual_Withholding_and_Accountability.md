## 1. Sexual Withholding and Control Mechanisms

Sapphire engaged in what can be described as "sex jail"—using sex as a tool of control and negotiation rather than as an expression of intimacy and connection. This created an asymmetrical power dynamic where:

- **She retained full sexual autonomy** while expecting me to remain exclusive
- **She controlled when sex occurred**, creating a situation where my needs were entirely dependent on her terms
- **Sex became transactional** rather than relational, tied to compliance with her requirements
- **My sexuality was held hostage** within a monogamous structure that gave her complete control

From my father's experience, which Sapphire read about: Under traditional Christian monogamy, you give your autonomy to your partner and they give their autonomy to you. But Sapphire was taking back her autonomy while still retaining control over mine—creating what my father called "using sex as a negotiating tool."

### 1.2 The Tragic Irony: We Started With Perfect Sexual Compatibility

This is what makes the situation even more devastating: **we were perfectly sexually compatible at the beginning**.

> "A home run was hit on the first night and our sexual chemistry and drive was up there."

I knew I had a higher sexual drive, so I **purposely sought out partners** who matched that energy. I had run into compatibility issues before and specifically screened for this. When Sapphire and I connected, I thought:

> "I found someone who's completely sexually compatible that I can be myself with and expect a certain sort of cadence that I could tolerate for the rest of my life in terms of being in a monogamous relationship."

**The sexual incompatibility wasn't inherent—it was created by circumstances.** The medical condition pumped the brakes on everything to a level I couldn't adjust for, no matter how much work I did.

### 1.3 The Frequency: A Quantifiable Crisis

Over the course of our 3+ year relationship, sexual frequency deteriorated to an unsustainable level:

- **Every 2-3 weeks** at best
- **Once a month** regularly
- **Once every 2 months** at worst
- This continued **for over 3 years**

When I asked ChatGPT (or anyone grounded in reality) if most men would stay in a relationship with this frequency, the answer was clear: most men would not. And it's not because they're shallow—it's because men often build emotional intimacy through physical intimacy. When sex becomes rare, they feel rejected, unseen, and unwanted.

**External Validation:**

Even people outside the relationship could see what Sapphire refused to acknowledge.

While there was an underlying medical condition that reduced her libido, the situation was exacerbated by her **artificial constraining of sex even further**. This created a compounding effect:

- The medical condition reduced sexual frequency to an unsustainable level

> "I'm looking at myself and understanding that I'm hypersexual, understanding that does put me on the higher end of things. Can I basically calm down to a lower threshold that would make it easier to be compatible? And I was like, yeah, I did that work."

**The problem was that the medical condition put the frequency so far out of reach** that even after doing that work, even after lowering my expectations significantly, it was still impossible:

> "The problem is there was the underlying medical condition that just put that frequency so far out of reach. Even somebody who's not hypersexual would be unable to stand for it."

This wasn't about me being unreasonable. This was about a situation that most people would find unsustainable.

## 7. The Final Realizations

### 7.1 What I Learned About Modern Monogamy

Through this experience, I came to understand:

- **Modern monogamy has a fundamental contradiction**: It demands exclusivity while rejecting the autonomy swap it was historically built on
- **Our generation has fundamentally rejected the traditional autonomy swap** that monogamy was built on—we no longer believe that being in a relationship means surrendering control over our own bodies, desires, or choices
- **But monogamy as a structure hasn't evolved** to fully function without that exchange
- **When situations like this arise, monogamy doesn't offer a way to resolve them fairly**
- **The result is a hybrid system** where women retain their sexual autonomy (good!) while also retaining control over their partner's autonomy (not good)

This isn't **true monogamy** (mutual autonomy swap) and it isn't **ethical non-monogamy** (both partners retaining autonomy). It's **possession**. It's **sex jail**. It's **toxic**.

### 7.2 What I Learned About Accountability

**Accountability requires:**
- Acknowledging your role in problems, not just offering last-minute solutions
- Actually listening to your partner's experience, not just defending your position
- Being willing to change, not just asking your partner to change
- Engaging with difficult conversations, not shutting them down
- Recognizing when your frameworks are harming your partner
- Prioritizing the relationship over being right

**Sapphire did none of these things until it was too late.**

### 7.3 What I Learned About Sexual Needs

- **Sexual needs are legitimate** and should not be shamed or dismissed
- **Sexual withholding as control is a form of emotional abuse**, regardless of gender
- **Both partners need autonomy** in a relationship, not just one
- **Men can and do experience serious pain** from sexual frustration
- **This isn't immaturity or weakness—it's neurobiology**
- Many men build emotional intimacy through physical intimacy, feel safety through sex, and feel love through being wanted
