### Gap 1 — Global Durability Objection: China, OPEC, and the Asian Tigers
**File:** `Paper/Redefining_Racism.tex`, after the Imperial Core Theorem definition (after the `\end{definition}` at ~line 8795)

**Why it matters:** The Imperial Core Theorem's claim — "peripheral liberation is neutralized unless Condition 1 or Condition 2 is met" — is directly challenged by three cases Grok and future peer reviewers will cite. The text currently says "The AES bloc represents the most significant contemporary test of Condition 2" but does not run the harder historical cases through the two-part test.

**The three cases to address:**

**China (1978–present):** The single strongest apparent counter-case. China moved from peripheral debtor nation under IMF/World Bank structural adjustment pressure to the world's second-largest economy with $3+ trillion in reserves, manufacturing sovereignty, and an alternative financial architecture (AIIB, Belt and Road, renminbi settlement). The framework's response:
- China's rise was enabled by $E_{\text{global}}$ deliberately offshoring manufacturing (labor arbitrage = $\max$ optimization, not $O_{\text{global}}$ empowerment)
- China's partial escape required replicating the extraction kernel internally (Uyghur extraction, internal migrant labor under hukou) — it escaped the Atlantic hierarchy by installing a parallel one
- Condition 2 is being tested: 40 years, nuclear deterrence, population scale, and $3T+ in reserves; no other peripheral actor commands these resources
- $\Delta\max = 0$ at the global apex: Chinese manufacturing wealth flows through the same global capital markets ($E_{\text{global}}$'s financial infrastructure)

**OPEC 1973 oil embargo:** Temporarily altered extraction terms — oil-producing periphery forced wealth transfer to core consumers. The framework's response:
- Absorbed through petrodollar recycling: Kissinger's 1974 deal required Saudi Arabia to denominate oil sales in dollars and reinvest surplus in US Treasuries, redirecting the wealth transfer back through $E_{\text{global}}$'s financial infrastructure within 24 months
- $\Delta\max = 0$: the extraction kernel adapted its interface (oil pricing in dollars, dollar reserve requirement) and the wealth flow resumed

**Asian Tigers (South Korea, Taiwan, Singapore):** Achieved genuine convergence to high-income status. The framework's response:
- International scope-condition cases: no intra-national racial partition was installed; colonial form was inter-national (Japanese extraction from Korea as a national unit), so without $\psi$, the developmental coalition could not be fractured
- Each was a Cold War client state receiving deliberate US investment as a geopolitical buffer — "development" was $E_{\text{global}}$ building buffer-class material wages at the national scale to prevent Condition 2 from forming organically on the Chinese model
- Singapore retains the highest Gini coefficient in Southeast Asia; South Korea's chaebol system concentrates extraction at the apex; $\Delta\max = 0$ at the domestic apex in each case

**Fix:** Add `\subsection{The Global Durability Objection: China, OPEC, and the Asian Tigers}` after the Imperial Core Theorem definition box (after the AES Bloc paragraph at ~line 8799), applying the two-part $\Delta\max = 0$ test.

### Gap 2 — Non-Western Imperial Systems Scope Condition
**File:** `Paper/Redefining_Racism.tex`, end of Section 13.1 (International 5-Tier Hierarchy), before the `\section{The Imperial Extraction Archive}` heading (~line 8539)

**Why it matters:** The global claim explicitly extends to "the modern world-system" — which includes Chinese, Ottoman, Mughal, and pre-colonial African systems that operated on genuinely different architectures and that the 5-tier hierarchy was not designed to model.

**Scope condition content:**
- The 5-tier hierarchy specifically models the **Atlantic-origin extraction architecture** (Lisbon 1453 → colonial empires → Bretton Woods 1944 → current dollar/military/institutional system) and its direct institutional successors
- Ottoman, Chinese tributary, Mughal, and pre-colonial African extraction systems operated without the racial partition ($\psi$) mechanism — they used conquest, tribute, and co-optation of local elites rather than a psychologically-waged buffer class
- These systems are outside the framework's **scope condition**: the 5-tier hierarchy applies where an intra-national or intra-imperial racial partition was installed as the primary class-fragmentation mechanism
- The contemporary global hierarchy (IMF, WTO, dollar system, NATO) is directly descended from the Atlantic colonial architecture — not from Ottoman, Chinese, or Mughal models — and this is what the chapter analyzes
- China is the boundary case: it experienced the Atlantic-origin hierarchy as an $O_{\text{global}}$ actor from the Opium Wars through structural adjustment, then partially escaped it, making it subject to the framework's analysis from within, not from outside as an alternative system

**Fix:** Add a `\paragraph{Scope condition: the Atlantic-origin constraint.}` at the end of Section 13.1, before the Imperial Extraction Archive section.

### Gap 3 — UN Vote Competing Geopolitical Drivers

- This is overdetermination: when multiple independent causal pathways converge on the same prediction, the prediction is more robust, not less
- The 5-tier framework subsumes these competing explanations: US-China rivalry IS the $E_{\text{global}}$ containment architecture; EU ambiguity IS $I_{\text{buffer}}^{\text{global}}$ protecting the infrastructure through inaction
