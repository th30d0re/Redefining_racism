# Integrating *The Delectable Negro* into Redefining_Racism.tex

## Diagnostic: What Woodard Adds That the Paper Does Not Yet Have

The paper currently treats extraction in three phases: **living (breeding, medical)** → **dead (ghost value, anthropodermic trade)**. Woodard documents a missing middle phase: the **literal and libidinal consumption of the living captive**, plus the **African counter-archive** that diagnosed the algorithm from first contact. The paper also uses "cannibalization" as a *metaphor* for intra-class consumption (5-Tier model) but never grounds it in the documented literal cannibalism Woodard recovers. Woodard supplies:

- Literal in-vivo consumption testimony (John S. Jacobs's "human fleshmongers," Northup's "gastronomical enjoyments," the 1838 *Essex* whaleship, the WPA "Master . . . eated me when I was meat" transcripts)
- Ritualized forced ingestion of **Nat Turner's boiled-down flesh** as "medicine" — a vector beyond the skin-purse/skull-trophy the paper already cites ([Paper/Redefining_Racism.tex](Paper/Redefining_Racism.tex) lines 2161–2169)
- The African cannibalism counter-archive (Bakongo, Igbo, Fanti, Bornu; Ali Eisami's 1818 narrative; 1911 *Le Rire* and 1937 *Hooger Leven* cartoons)
- Samuel A. Cartwright's "edible/childlike Negro" taxonomy as a polymorphic interface converting extraction-appetite into gastronomic "taste"
- Institutionalized hunger as a discrete control subroutine (the Somerset Place anecdote) — a direct calibration of the paper's `Min` variable below subsistence
- The homoerotic dimension of white-on-Black male domination, including the "male daughter / male womb / male mother" recovery motif in diasporic slave narratives — a reprise, in captivity, of the Igbo male-daughter institution the paper already cites ethnographically at line 1592
- Orlando Patterson's **parasite** thesis (*Slavery and Social Death*) as theoretical predecessor to the paper's `extraction_kernel` framing

### Insert 4 — Ch. 6 "Gendered Axis" — NEW SUBSECTION (the flagship insert)

**Target:** [Paper/Redefining_Racism.tex](Paper/Redefining_Racism.tex) between §`The Completeness Theorem` (line 2179–2188) and §`The Medical Extraction Laboratory` (line 2190).

**New subsection heading:**
```
\subsection{The In-Vivo Subroutine: \texttt{libidinal\_extraction.exe} and the Gastronomic Partition}
\label{subsec:libidinal_extraction}
```

**Structure (approx. 4–5 paragraphs):**

1. **Opening the evidentiary gap.** The Completeness Theorem states that extraction is *temporally* complete. The three preceding subsections (teeth, skin, hair) demonstrate extraction on the post-mortem body; the following section (Medical Laboratory) demonstrates extraction on the experimental body. There remains a documented archive, long suppressed, of extraction on the **living, eating, erotic** body. Introduce this as the \texttt{libidinal\_extraction.exe} subroutine. Cite Woodard as the primary archival historian and Patterson's parasite thesis as the theoretical ground.

2. **The literal-consumption archive.** John S. Jacobs's "human fleshmongers"; Solomon Northup's description of slaveholders' "gastronomical enjoyments"; Harriet Jacobs's accounts of white mistresses acting out consumptive hungers in intimate domestic space; the 1838 whaleship *Essex*, where the first four crew members murdered and eaten were Black and were treated under the master/slave ideology even in a nominally Quaker-abolitionist Nantucket context; WPA interviews containing the testimony "Master . . . eated me when I was meat." Frame: not rhetorical flourishes, not Southern Gothic coloration, but first-person testimony the nineteenth-century whites consistently dismissed as "folk belief" and twentieth-century scholars largely continued to dismiss.

3. **Institutionalized hunger as Min-calibration.** The Somerset Place anecdote (Josiah Collins III plantation, NC): the peck of corn and three pounds of meat per month against which field labor could not subsist. Hunger as a discrete control subroutine, not a byproduct — a deliberate depression of the Out-group's life-support below biological sufficiency, which the paper's existing `Min` variable already predicts. The master is the parasite who *feeds on the hunger he has engineered*.

4. **The gastronomic partition and the Master Epicure.** Cartwright's edibility-taxonomy (referenced in Insert 1) combined with Woodard's evidence of the "Master Epicure" gourmand aesthetic. This is the polymorphic interface by which extraction is experienced, by the Elite, as *refined taste* — the aestheticization of dominance that the Roman penetration hierarchy (§4.8 Node 1) had prototyped and that Augustinian shame (Node 3) theologized. This is where libidinal_extraction.exe interfaces with the Roman/Augustinian ideological archive already mapped in the paper.

5. **The homoerotic subroutine.** Woodard's central archival discovery: the homoerotic charge of white male consumption of Black male captivity is not an interpretive overlay but is documented in slave narratives, WPA transcripts, and the colonial visual archive (the *Le Rire* and *Hooger Leven* images, the "missionary position" of the skewered Bakongo man). Douglass's *Narrative* and *My Bondage and My Freedom* read, in Woodard's hands, as a ravishment-hunger dialectic: the enslaved body is made hungry and then made desirous, the master's appetite and the captive's starvation are the same transaction viewed from opposite sides. Frame this as the literal-archival confirmation of the **Masculine Primal Wound** already developed at §4.9 (line 2318) — Black masculine "structural emasculation under demand to perform" is diagnosable in the 19th-century record precisely because the enslaved were describing it in the symbolic-reproductive terms the paper now reintroduces.

6. **Closing: subroutine registration.** A short paragraph formally registering `libidinal_extraction.exe` in the paper's subroutine taxonomy alongside `biological_extraction.exe`, `material_extraction.exe`, and `scientific_extraction.exe`, and noting that the four subroutines are jointly exhaustive of the temporal and material extent of the captive body. This closes the Completeness Theorem with literal, not metaphorical, totality.

### Insert 5 — Ch. 6 "Gendered Axis" — Nat Turner subsection augmentation

**Target:** [Paper/Redefining_Racism.tex](Paper/Redefining_Racism.tex) §`Skin: Nat Turner and the Anthropodermic Trade` (lines 2161–2168).

**Add:** one short paragraph, inserted before the final framework-reading paragraph, documenting the **ritualized forced ingestion of Turner's boiled-down flesh**. Woodard recovers WPA oral-history evidence from Black members of the Southampton, Virginia community that whites attempted to coerce Black persons into consuming Turner's rendered flesh and entrails, and that Turner was allegedly consumed by whites as a medicinal substance. Nineteenth- and twentieth-century scholars dismissed this as "folk belief of older darkies." The paper's framework reading: consumption-as-counter-revolution. The state's response to anti-systemic violence is not only commodity-ization of the corpse (purses, grease, trophy bones — already cited) but **ritualized oral incorporation** of the rebel body as anti-rebel medicine. This is the gastronomic complement to the anthropodermic trade.

This is the paper's thesis written by the enslaved themselves: the structurally emasculated Out-group turns to the gender-flexible African kinship archive (Ch. 5 §5.3) as a recovery technology against libidinal extraction. It is the continuity the paper's current Masculine Primal Wound section asserts analytically but does not yet demonstrate textually.
